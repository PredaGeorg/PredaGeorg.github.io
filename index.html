George Fearnley

Welcome to my webpage! I recently graduated from Durham University with a master's degree in Physics. I am currently working as an online tutor for MyTutor while looking for graduate roles in technology.

Education

After studying Maths, Physics and Chemistry at school I went on to study Physics at Durham University. I loved my time there. The people were great, the city is beautiful and the course was interesting.

I particularly enjoyed the first year 'Introduction to Programming' module. The final project was to write a programme that traced the path of light rays through media of different shapes and different refractive indices. After that point, at every opportunity I opted for modules that had a programming element. Inspired by Breakthrough Starshot, my final year project was to computatioanlly model a novel approach to rocketry.

Recently I have been using Cousera to teach myself web development HTML5, CSS3 and JavaScript. In fact, I have used what I learned to build this website!

Experience

I am currently working as an online tutor for MyTutor and to date I have taught over 250 classes (including some private tuition).

After graduating in 2019 I spent 7 months in Bolivia working for the Bolivian Express: a free magazine that seeks to promote cultural exchange between English speaking countries and the Bolivia. I wrote articles on topics in science and gastronomy, and worked hard on my Spanish to achieve professional proficiency (B2/C1).

In the summer of 2018 I was awarded a summer research position within Simulations Of Soft Matter Group, lead by Dr Halim Kusumaatmaja, looking into the phenomenon of cylindrical buckling. Throughout the project I gained experience with MATLAB, bash scripting and data visualisation.

Earlier that summer I also completed at teaching internship at Heworth Grange school in Gateshead.
